Corvette C3

1968

  -1982

Named the "Shark", the C3 Corvette was met with mixed reviews. The '68 and '69 models were essentially unchanged from its predecessor. This generation saw the arrival of the ZL-1 which originally made 430 hp but could make up to 580 hp with the stock manifolds scrapped for headers. This generation saw the emergence of wild engines in which racing enthusiasts could essentially buy a factory race-ready sports car.

Yearly Changes

1968 Completely restyled features an industry first "T-top"; Lines closely resemble the Mako Shark II show car; headlamps are now backlit "pop-up" designs
1969 The 250,000th Corvette - a gold convertible - comes off of the St. Louis production line in November; "Stingray" script is added about the fender; ZR-1 optional factory-installed racking package is offered
1970 New LT1 Small Block V8 engine option with solid lifters is introduced at 370 hp
1971 Essentially remained unchanged from 1970
1972 Last year to feature both front and rear chrome bumpers, a bright egg-crate grill and side-fender grills
1973 4,000 serial numbers were never built, so the production totaled 30,464
1974 Last year of the 454 big-block
1975 Final year of the convertible until its 1986 return
1976 An over-the-radiator, carburetor air-induction system is a new standard feature with a partial steel underbody is added to add strength
1977 The 500,000th Corvette - a white coupe with red interior - is produced in St. Louis in March
1978 Fastback body style marks Corvette's 25th year of production; traditional crossed-flag emblem is replaced with a special anniversary emblem; special edition Indy 500 Pace Car replica
1979 Halogen headlamps become available for the first time
1980 New front and rear plastic bumper caps with integrated spoilers and new hood improved drag coefficient
1981 Mid-year production shifted to Bowling Green, KY - first time a model was built in two locations simultaneously
1982 Convenient hatchback design introduced; four-speed automatic transmissions with overdrive became standard with not manual transmission offered until 1984

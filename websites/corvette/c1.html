Corvette C1

1953

  -1962

Designed by Harley Earl, the concept Corvette was an amazing success during its debut at the Motorama. With the raving responses, Chevrolet began building the Corvette as soon as possible. It took GM six months to begin producing the first cars and rolled them out later that same year. The car was masculine and muscular, taking America by storm. The C1 was the grand beginning of America's Sports car.

Yearly Changes

1953 Polo white with red interior and black top is the only color combination
1954 Blue, red, and black are added with a beige top and longer exhaust pipes
1955 Straight-6 and 265ci V8 engines produced and 3-speed manual is introduced
1956 New body with roll-up windows and 3-speed manual becomes standard
1957 283ci V8 and optional 4-speed manual and fuel injection added
1958 Quad-headlight body and new interior
1959 First black interior and dash storage bin; only year with turquoise top
1960 Red and blue bars on dash logo
1961 New rear styling, bumpers and round taillights
1962 327ci V8 engine and last year with trunk until 1998

Find Your Adventure

Discover

Calaveras became a State Park in 1931 to preserve the North Grove of giant sequoias. This grove includes the "Discovery Tree", also known as the "Big Stump", the first Sierra redwood noted by Augustus T. Dowd in 1852. This area has been a major tourist attraction ever since, and is considered the longest continuously operated tourist facility in California.

Over the years, other parcels of mixed conifer forests have been added to the park. This amazing park has a huge varitey of habitats and recreational opportunites to explore.

Seasons in the Calaveras

During the Summer enjoy evening ranger talks, numerous interpretive programs, environmental educational programs, junior ranger programs, hiking, mountain biking, bird watching and activities for school children. In the spring, view the spectacular mountain dogwoods in bloom in the North Grove. The sight of all the dogwoods in bloom contrasted against the forest and giant redwoods is a sight unparalleled. Road access to the South Grove, Stanislaus River and Bradley Grove are not accessible in the winter months. However, even though roads are closed, cross country skiing is available and encouraged in the Park during the winter months.

Things to Do

Explore the park through various activites and hot spots. The park is open year-round from sunrise to sunset. From March to November, camping is an excellent option to view everything the park has to offer. With access to the beach and water, we offer fishing and swimming areas near the hiking trails. In between hikes, families can enjoy our picnic areas or family programs. In addition to all of these features, below are some of the more popular attractions at the park.

Hours: 10 a.m. - 4 p.m.

Come visit our gift shop and mueseum where you can cool down after your hike. Here you are able to really see the wildlife in the park come to life as a theater plays two short videos on loop that are both educational and enjoyable for all ages.

Length: 1.5 miles

On Saturdays at 1:00 p.m., come and enjoy this unique experience through the North Grove area of the park. With paid admission to the park the cost of the hike is free to all family members (except dogs). The hike will be a snowshoe hike if weather permits it.

Length: 5 miles

A beautiful hike through the giant sequoias in their natural habitat. Enjoy this free hike year-round while the park is open.

Places to Stay

The park is northeast of Stockton, four miles northeast of Arnold on Highway 4. Calaveras Big Tree State Park offers two different accomodations on the premises. There are several hotels located in Arnold, CA if any of these accomodations don't fit a visitor's stay.

Campgrounds

The park offers two main campgrounds with 129 campsites total, six picnic areas and several miles of trails. Visitors will be able to reserve campsites and lodging six months in advance from the current date. Bookings may extend from the arrival date to the desired departure date – based on availability and the park’s maximum stay rules. Please call to inquire about availabilities and reservations.

Cabins

There are four rental cabins offered to those who want to stay the night but not camp outside. The cabins have two bedrooms, a private bathroom and a kitchen. This is the perfect place to stargaze and sit around the campfire creating unforgettable memories. These cabins offer a unique and historical place to stay just a short walk from the North Grove Trail. Please call to inquire about availabilities and reservations.

Directions

Take I-580 eastbound over Altamont Pass to I-205 toward Manteca, to US 99 North. Take the exit for State Hwy 4 Eastbound (Angel's Camp) to the Park Entrance. Calaveras Big Trees is about 35 minutes driving from Angel's Camp.

Take either I-5 or US 99 North. From I-5 you can cross to the other side of Stockton on State Hwy 4 to 99/4 South a few miles, then follow Hwy 4 towards and beyond Farmington to the Park. Calaveras Big Trees is about 35 minutes driving time from Angel's Camp.

Take U.S. 99 South to Stockton. Turn East on State Hwy 4. Driving time to the park from here is approx 1 hour and 30 minutes. Pass through Farmington and Copperopolis to the Park. An alternate route from Sacramento is to take State Hwy 16 to State Hwy 49 South through Jackson, San Andreas, and Angels Camp.

Take US 395 to State Hwy 89 West to the terminus of State Hwy 4, up over Ebbett's Pass to the Park. The road is closed in Winter. It's very scenic, but so steep and tortuous that trailers and large motorhomes are ill-advised to use it.

Please note that many car navigation systems and handheld Global Positioning Devices (GPS) are unable to locate the park.

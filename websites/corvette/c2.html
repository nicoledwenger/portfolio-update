Corvette C2

1963

  -1967

This generation was born from a handful of experimental machines that designers and engineers Zora Arkus-Duntov and Bill Mitchell had devised. Independent rear suspension was a large mechanical change that shapes American cars for years to come. The split window would remain to be one of the most controversial design elements of its generation; however, nowadays, this feature is sought after by Corvette enthusiasts.

Yearly Changes

1963 Completely restyled body based on Bill Mitchell's 1959 Sting Ray race car. First year of the Corvette coupe and only year with the split-window coupe; The Z06 is offered as an option on the 1963 Stingray (199 built)
1964 The split-window design is removed because it inhibited the driver's rearward vision
1965 Big Block V8 engines were introduced with the 396 CID L78 option at 425 hp
1966 Factory-installed driver/passenger headrests as optional; first year with the 427 CID engine at 425 hp; Holley Carburetors were installed in all engines
1967 Optional L88 engine offered (20 produced); energy-absorbing steering column, four-way hazard warning flashers and a dual master cylinder brake system were standard; exterior trim removed or restyled as well as the hood and fender vents

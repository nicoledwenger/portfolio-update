Corvette C6

2005

  -2013

The engineers and designers, led by Dave Hill, weren't tasked with a full redesign of this generation, but rather to change the current design and simply make it better. Most changes revolved around the aesthetics of the car in the interior and exterior; however, the engine was given a performance boost. The performance and design of the sixth generation helped reestablish the Corvette name as America's sports car.

Yearly Changes

2005 Powered by a new 6.0L small block V8 making 400 hp with exposed headlights and power top option plus OnStar and navigation available for the first time
2006 Z06 returned with a 7.0L small block V8 introduced that produced 505 hp and 470 lb-ft torque and a six-speed paddle shift automatic transmission was introduced
2007 Ron Fellows Championship Edition is the first signed limited edition in Corvette history
2008 LS3 6.2L base V8 engine with a performance increase to 430 hp plus the introduction of the leather-wrapped interior option
2009 Debut of the new Corvette ZR1 powered by a super-charged 6.2L V8 introduced making 638 hp
2010 Grand Sport model returns with the LS3 engine and Z06 chassis and side air bags were standard on all models
2011 Exteriors limited to Supersonic Blue or Inferno Orange
2012 Chevrolet Centennial Edition (available on all models) features Carbon Flash metallic paint, satin-black graphics, satin-black cup wheels with red stripe, unique badges and special trim with red stitching
2013 60th anniversary and 427 Convertible Collector Edition produced
